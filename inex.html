Drops of Change

Islamic Educational College (IEC Schools) — Amman, Jordan

Introduction

We, students from IEC Schools in Amman, Jordan, believe in turning every drop of rain into a resource. Our project “Drops of Change” proposes repairing our school’s ceiling and installing a rainwater harvesting system. By collecting rainwater, we can save up to 60% of our water use, reduce waste, and create an educational example for other schools in Jordan.

How It Works

  1. Repair roof/ceiling and install gutters to collect rain.
  2. Use a first-flush diverter and mesh filter to remove debris.
  3. Store water in a 1,500 L (pilot) tank for non-potable uses.
  4. Use collected water for gardening, cleaning, and toilets.

Impact & Numbers

  • Example harvest: 200 m² roof × 600 mm/year → ~120,000 L/year.
  • Non-potable coverage in good setups: 50%–80%.
  • Typical potable savings range: 10%–35%.

Future Goals

Our vision for Drops of Change is to expand beyond IEC Schools and inspire other schools across Jordan to adopt similar rainwater harvesting systems. By turning every drop into a resource, we hope to reduce water waste, raise environmental awareness, and create a culture of sustainability that helps both our communities and the planet.
